Divisions of Bangladesh

Dhaka Division

Dhaka is the capital city of Bangladesh, known for its vibrant economy, bustling streets, and rich cultural heritage. It is the political, economic, and cultural center of the country, home to historical landmarks, diverse cuisine, and the bustling Dhaka University.

Chattogram Division

Chattogram, also known as Chittagong, is a major coastal city in Bangladesh, renowned for its bustling seaport, which is the largest in the country. It is a gateway to the Bay of Bengal, famous for its ship-breaking yards, scenic beaches, and the hill tracts region.

Khulna Division

Khulna is the gateway to the Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is famous for its shrimp farming industry and rich biodiversity. The city also has significant industrial and commercial activities.

Barishal Division

Barishal, situated in the south-central region of Bangladesh, is known for its picturesque rivers and waterways, often referred to as the "Venice of the East." It is famous for floating markets and guava orchards. The region's natural beauty attracts many tourists.

Sylhet Division

Sylhet is famous for its tea gardens, lush green landscapes, and natural beauty. It is also known for the Sylhet region's significant expatriate population, especially in the UK. The city is a gateway to numerous natural attractions, including Jaflong and Ratargul Swamp Forest.

Rangpur Division

Rangpur is an important agricultural hub in northern Bangladesh, known for its rice, jute, and potato production. The division is also home to ancient archaeological sites and rich cultural heritage. The city of Rangpur is a center for trade and education.

Mymensingh Division

Mymensingh is known for its educational institutions, including the Bangladesh Agricultural University. The division is located along the Brahmaputra River and is famous for its lush green landscapes, cultural heritage, and traditional crafts. The area is agriculturally rich.
